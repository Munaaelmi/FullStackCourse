This recipe creates decadent brownies that have the perfect balance between a fudgy, moist interior and a crackly, shiny top that adds

Today, we're making the most perfect fudge brownies that offer a delightful balance of sweetness and decadence, with a beautiful velvety
taste and texture that is truly irresistible, and the best part is that they are super simple to make!

Few tips:

  • If you want to go above and beyond with your brownies; Start with high-quality ingredients like premium chocolate, unsweetened cocoa powder,
    and good-quality butter. The quality of these ingredients will greatly impact the final taste and texture of your brownies.

  • Accurately measuring your ingredients is very important in achieving perfect fudge brownies.

  • Melt the chocolate and butter gently: When melting the chocolate and butter, do it slowly and gently, on low-medium heat. Be cautious not to
    overheat or burn them, as it can affect the texture of the brownies.

  • Once you add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mix just until everything is combined. Over-mixing can result in a denser and tougher brownie.

  • To achieve the best outcome, it is vital to let the brownies cool to room temperature before cutting into them, as they continue to cook in the remaining heat of the pan.

PREP TIME

15 mins

COOK TIME

30 mins

Total TIME

45 mins

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 1/2 cups Semi-sweet chocolate chips - 250 grams
  • 12 tbps Unsalted butter, chopped - 175 grams
  • 1/3 cup Unsweeted cocoa powder - 42 grams
  • 1 tsp Espresso powder
  • 1/4 tsp Cinnamon *optional* but I highly recommend as cinnamon, in my opinion, compliments chocolate very well.
  • 3 Eggs 155 grams
  • 1 1/2 cups Sugar - 310 grams
  • 2 tbsp Brown sugar, packed - 34 grams
  • 1 1/2 tsp Vanilla extract - 8 ml
  • 3/4 cups All-purpose flour - 125 grams
  • 2 tbsp Dry milk - 12 grams *optional*
  • 1 tsp Salt

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C)
  2. In a medium saucepan set over low-medium heat, melt the butter and chocolate together gently. While your chocolate and butter are slowly melting, mix together you cocoa powder, cinnamon, and espresso in a small bowl.
  3. Once your chocolate and butter are melted, turn off the heat and immediately whisk in the cocoa powder mixture.
  4. In a separate mixing bowl, utilize a hand mixer or a stand mixer equipped with the paddle attachment to beat the eggs, sugars, and vanilla extract together for about 4 minutes until light, fluffy, and slightly thickened. After this duration, if you were to rub the mixture between your fingers, there should be almost no detectable granules of sugar remaining.
  5. Slowly mix the melted chocolate mixture into the egg and sugar mixture, ensuring they are thoroughly combined.
  6. Add the flour, dry milk, and salt to the mixture and mix together just until there are no visible streaks of flour remaining. Avoid over-mixing to maintain a tender texture.
  7. This is also where you can throw in any ad-ins! (extra chocolate chips for example)
  8. Pour the brownie batter into a prepared 8 inch square pan, spreading it evenly. If you don't have a non-stick pan, i highly encourage you to line your pan with parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  9. Bake the brownies in the preheated oven for approximately 30 minutes, or until the edges are set and the center of the brownies slightly jiggles when gently shaken.
  10. Once baked, remove the pan from the oven and allow the brownies to cool completely in the pan. This cooling process will help them set properly and develop their fudgy texture.
  11. Once cooled, carefully lift the brownies out of the pan using the parchment paper as handles (or flip them over gently if you used a trustworthy non-stick pan) . Place them on a cutting board and slice into desired portions.
  12. Now, your perfectly baked fudge brownies are ready to be enjoyed! Serve them as is or with a dusting of powdered sugar, a scoop of ice cream, or any other desired toppings.
